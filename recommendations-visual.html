Ideation
Close the External AI Gap
Users start in ChatGPT or Gemini for presentation outlines and content structuring. The gap isn't design inspiration — Canva's library handles that. It's structured content planning.
"I need you to make this more fun… put a spin on this" — P6, to ChatGPT, not Canva.

Manual Valley
The middle
Fill the AI Desert
Zero AI presence in the middle of every workflow. Users default to manual because AI doesn't surface there. Introduce lightweight micro-assists — smart alignment, layout suggestions, colour harmonisation — without requiring users to seek them.
"Assuming I can do most things on my own without AI… I use it less." — P2

AI Features
Entry points
Lower the Bar for "Capable"
Not education — reduce what "capable" means. Pre-built prompts, contextual suggestions, one-click actions. Recognise prompt content and surface quick-apply: "match my style," "apply brand." UT-P10 didn't know Canva AI existed; UT-P7/P8 gave up entirely.
"It's way easier to just find the template and add the colors myself than it is to prompt." — P1

End of Session
Polish & finishing
Position AI as Easier, Not More Powerful
Don't position AI as powerful — position it as easier. End of session, Canva has context about what's been built. Suggest finishing touches: "add flourishes," "tidy alignment," "spell check." Users see AI as one intelligent system — lean into that.
"Going with what you know and the path of least resistance." — P4
